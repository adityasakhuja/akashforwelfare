4 years doing Physics, International student, RCSU Welfare Officer for 2 years, naive football player and amateur stand-up comedian. But that’s not enough. While I love being a part of Imperial, the student satisfaction is appalling. The primary reason being that Imperial puts us through vigorous, unfamiliar and sometimes harsh situations. While this helps us become leaders in tomorrow’s world, it does not feel pleasant today. This is irrespective of what you study and what level you are studying at. That’s why I am taking a stand.

Here are my game plans:

  • Raise awareness among students about current College support system
  • Increase College support effectiveness, by bringing together personal tutors, (mums and dads?), department representatives, welfare officers from all unions (somewhere centralized) to make your life easier and manageable
  • Improve Postgraduate participation throughout university – a significant, more mature student body we barely get the opportunity to learn from
  • Help push accommodation costs down, for affordable living near college and preventing any more hall closures
  • Advocate the need for increased mental health awareness, so that students know how the college helps in tackling it
  • Prioritize support for student-led campaigns like Mentality and ICSexism, so that your voice and concerns are heard and acted upon
  • Be available in the JCR/SCR once every week, to know you can count on and question me
  • Host a welfare open forum once every term for you to voice your concerns
  • Most importantly, putting a smile on your face

Why me though?

  • I have lived through 4 years of lectures, exams, labs – I know stress and anxiety like I know my flatmates, annoying
  • I am an international student. I have lived far away from home, in seclusion, on a different continent, with a language that is not my mother tongue, just trying to fit in
  • I KNOW life can suck sometimes and it can be amazing at other times
  • Defeated a physical injury during first year exams, and extreme stress overload at the end of second year, to personally know there were much better ways to handle physical and mental health, especially during exam time
  • Welfare Officer for RCSU since 2014, dealing with student issues in Union Council, organizing and leading welfare week last year and another one upcoming this year, so keep a look out!
  • I want to help because I am one of you and I wish someone helped me
